Interesting Facts about Camel:-

  • Camels can live around 40 - 50 years.
  • Camels are smarter than horses.
  • Newborn camels don't have humps. They gradually grow as they get older.
  • Female camels are called cows and males are called bulls.

Interesting Facts about Chameleon:-

  • The panther chameleon changes its color to yellow or red when it’s angry.
  • Sick chameleons look pale in color.
  • There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  • The chameleons' eyes can rotate and focus separately on 180-degree arcs.

Interesting Facts about Cicada:-

  • Cicada can live for up to 17 years in the larva stage.
  • They have special straw-like mouths used to suck liquid from plant roots.
  • The female cicada lays 400 to 600 eggs.
  • About six to 10 weeks after the eggs are laid in cracks and holes in trees.

Interesting Facts about Panda:-

  • They are found in thick bamboo forests, high up in the mountains of central China
  • The giant panda’s scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means “black and white cat-foot”.
  • Giant pandas grow to between 1.2m and 1.5m, and weigh between 75kg and 135kg.
  • Female pandas give birth to one or two cubs every two years.

Interesting Facts about Pigeon:-

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet.

Interesting Facts about teddy-bear:-

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first British Teddy Bear Festival was held in 1989 in London.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
